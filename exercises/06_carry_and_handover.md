# Exercise 6 — Carry and Hand-Over

**Applies to:** RH – F, FL, T, L, MT, W; all levels  
**Points:** 10 p

## Setup
- 1 table or elevated place (for D to be picked up from)
- 1 marked spot for hand-over
- 1 marked spot for final position
- 1 assistant (HP — Hilfsperson)

## Permitted AC/VC (Level V)
- 1 AC and/or VC for jumping onto table (from GS)
- 1 AC or VC for recall
- 1 AC or VC for GS

## Execution — Level V
1. From GS, D jumps onto table at AC/VC.
2. DH picks up D from table, carries D 10 steps straight on.
3. **During carry: D's tail must be free.**
4. DH hands D over to HP.
5. HP carries D 10 steps next to DH, **D's head facing DH**. DH may speak to D.
6. HP puts D down; DH ends with GS.

## Execution — Level A and B
1–4 same as Level V.
5. HP carries D 10 steps **further** (away from DH); DH **stops and stays**.
6. D must **remain** with HP until DH calls D with 1 AC.
7. D comes quickly, sits in front, after 3 sec → GS on AC.

## Evaluation — Faults
- D not cooperative or not calm during carry → devalue accordingly
- D growls slightly or pulls back when picked up/put down → devalue
- **D jumps off during carry → Insufficient**
- D leaves HP after being put down but before being recalled (and returns) → **max Satisfactory** for that partial
